Corinne CharetteChief Information Officer Treasury Board Secretariat

About Corinne

Corinne Charette was appointed to the position of Chief Information Officer of the Government of Canada, effective May 4, 2009.

Ms. Charette came to the Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat from Transat A.T. Inc., where she was Vice-President and Chief Information Officer from 2006 until 2009. Previous to that, Ms. Charette was Deputy Director and Chief Information Officer of FINTRAC. During her professional career of more than 30 years, she served as Senior Vice-President, Internet Channel, for the Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce, was a Partner with KPMG Consulting, leading their e-Business practice, and worked for IBM Global Services and Via Rail Canada.

Ms. Charette holds a Bachelor of Science degree in engineering from Concordia University and is a professional engineer. On June 21, 2011, Ms. Charette received an honorary degree of Doctor of Laws from Concordia University, in recognition of her distinguished career and achievements.

@NeoInsight

Lisa FastSenior Partner, User Experience Research & Strategy NeoInsight

Building Usability Into Design

As a leading usability expert with decades of experience in the field, Lisa Fast from NeoInsight will speak about the role of usability in user-centred design. This session will include examples of how usability can be evaluated and some common usability failings.

About Lisa

Since joining Neo Insight in 2007, Lisa Fast has tested or interviewed over 500 usability participants and conducted expert reviews of 21 web sites. Those hours of watching participants perform web tasks gave her a keen eye for User Experience issues. Altogether, since her first usability testing experience in 1986, she has over 25 years of success in applying behavioral design techniques to the complete customer experience, including conceptual designs, usability testing, user interface design, innovative product development and applied cognitive research.

Lisa holds a Master’s degree in Computer Science, in the study of the psychology of programming. She held positions in usability testing, design management, product management, marketing and technology licensing at Nortel and Mitel. From 2003 to 2008, Lisa conducted qualitative and quantitative research at the Centre for Applied Cognitive Research at Carleton University, including a national survey on early numeracy in 2008.

Lisa holds two patents related to wireless communications (WO/ 2000/028762 and7103550). Her design publications are listed on interaction-design.org. Lisa is an active GitHub collaborator on the Government of Canada's Web Experience Toolkit (WET). She joined the Treasury Board WET team on a panel at GTEC 2012 to discuss ' Putting Canadians First Through Open Collaborative Web Development'. In March 2014, she spoke at the Information Architecture Summit in San Diego.

Pirth SinghDeputy Project Executive - Web Renewal Treasury Board Secretariat

About Pirth

Pirth Singh has a B. Eng. in Electrical Engineering and an MBA in Finance from McMaster University in Hamilton, Ontario. Over his fourteen years in the Government of Canada, Pirth has held positions in Policy Development, Business and Industry analysis and Information Technology at the Public Service Commission, Industry Canada, Royal Canadian Mounted Police and Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat.

He currently serves as the Deputy Project Executive for the Web Renewal Initiative and leads the Web Standards Office at Treasury Board Secretariat, which has been the highlight of his career. Through Pirth’s commitment to building a community of collaboration within and outside of the Government of Canada, he has been a catalyst for changing the way policy is developed and the way departments come together to address common challenges. Under Pirth’s leadership, his small team took up the challenge to work collaboratively with hundreds of individuals across many departments and several private sector organizations to ensure websites and web applications are accessible, usable, interoperable and optimized for mobile. Some examples of these collaborative initiatives include the Web Experience Toolkit, open collaboration on GitHub, Standard on Web Usability and of course CodeFest.

Pirth’s staff consider him their fearless leader, a great debater, mentor and a shining example of the leadership ideals required to make the public service an amazing place to work. Known for leaving his BlackBerry everywhere he goes, Pirth can most frequently be seen carrying a Starbucks coffee.

@HealthyCdns

Jane Hazel Director General of the Public Affairs Directorate Health Canada

The Evolution: From Healthy Canadians to the Health Theme

As the Government of Canada moves towards one website, management, writers, designers and developers are rethinking how Web content is developed. Jane Hazel, Director General of the Public Affairs Directorate at Health Canada, will share her experience moving from an organization-based website to a theme-based website. Learn from Health Canada’s experience before your department re-positions its business model to create a streamlined, mobile-first and user-centric web presence on Canada.ca.

About Jane

As Director General of the Public Affairs Directorate at Health Canada, Jane’s responsibilities include national social marketing plans and strategies, partnership development, public opinion research and consultation, leading on open government and overseeing the web presence for both Health Canada and the Public Health Agency of Canada.

Management of the Department’s web sites was centralized under communications and Healthy Canadians, the first themed web site bringing together content from multiple Departments, was launched several years ago. A new Recalls and Safety Alerts database was created in the fall of 2012 and has built on the success of the Healthy Canadians web presence. Through this centralization and theme-based approach to the site, Jane has led her team to effectively reduce web content to ensure a user-centric experience, and continually consults web metrics to drive the decision making process, all of which is critical to a successful web site.

With over 15 years of experience in public sector management, Jane’s focus on evidence-based decision making and marketing principles has contributed to the success of Health Canada in reaching Canadians with important health and safety information.

Jane holds an MBA from the University of Ottawa and a Bachelor’s of Science degree from McGill.

Alëna IouguinaUX Researcher Shopify

Integral UX Research – Shopify Perspective

Exploring theoretical foundations of UX research and practice as a highly complex synthesis of the beautiful – the art of design, the good – the ethics of design, and the true – the science of design. Cyd HarrellUX Evangelist Code for America

About Cyd

Cyd is a passionate advocate for the citizen experience. She's an accomplished user experience (UX) researcher and currently serves as Code for America's UX Evangelist. She's also a Twitter haikuist. Cyd wants to redesign interaction between government and its citizens to create a virtuous cycle of trust. She envisions a world where civic interactions are just as inviting and rewarding as commercial ones.

At Code for America, Cyd works with fellows, city officials, and community volunteers to help create inventive and cost-effective civic technology that serves the needs of real people. In 2014, she is working closely with the city of Oakland CA to redesign the redesign process for local government websites. She uses her unique background in sociolinguistics and poetry to apply metaphorical thinking to human-centered design.
